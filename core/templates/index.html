About

{% endfor %}

Troy Dixon is a Nashville-based audio engineer specialized in mixing and mastering. Dixon has over a decade of experience in the recording industry. His career began in Los Angeles working with hip hop/rap mix engineer Dave Aron. Working with Mr. Aron was formative for Dixon in learning how to build mixes with power and clarity. In LA, Dixon worked with many diverse acts ranging from dub reggae pioneer The Scientist to influential alternative-rock band Jane’s Addiction. Dixon now calls Nashville home and is focused on creating the best sounding mixes and masters possible.

The Space

The Golden Ratio is the guiding principle behind the dimensions and design of Dixon’s mastering studio. These dimensions allow for a natural, non-resonant, acoustic environment ideal for precise audio playback. Owens Corning 703 acoustic insulation is incorporated in floor to ceiling bass traps and side wall absorption and is presented in FR701 acoustic fabric from Guilford of Maine.



The Gear

A set of Tyler Acoustics D1X reference monitors, powered by a solid-state Bryston 4BST, fill the studio with full frequency playback. A secondary set of Wharfedale Valdus 500 monitors with Hafler amplification provide an additional point of reference. The monitoring chain relies on high resolution digital to analog conversion which is performed by the benchmark Crane Song Avocet II converter system. The highest quality audio processing is accomplished using software from iZotope, Universal Audio, HOFA, FabFilter, and others.
